This textbook on Bayesian latent class models was initially developed to support a workshop offered with my colleague Juan Carlos Arango Sabogal. Bayesian statistical methods have been widely applied in veterinary science and epidemiological research. In particular, Bayesian latent class variable models (LCM) have been shown to be useful for estimating disease prevalence or diagnostic test sensitivity and specificity in the absence of a gold standard test. In this document we introduce these methods and illustrate their application using R and JAGS with concrete veterinary examples. There is also a series of accompanying exercises (named Exercise 1 - Proportions to Exercise 6 - Sample size estimation and study design) wich can be downloaded from the course repository on my Github account.
